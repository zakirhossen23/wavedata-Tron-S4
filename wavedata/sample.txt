The Medical Outcomes Study (MOS) was a 2-year observational study of patients with chronic conditions, conducted in a cross-sectional phase among 22,462 patients and in a longitudinal phase among 2349 patients (Tarlov et al. 1989). One of its two main purposes was to analyze how outcomes vary depending on factors such as the type of delivery system in which a patient receives care, clinician specialty training, and intensity of resource use.
